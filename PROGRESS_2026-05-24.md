State of the LatentCityGPT project at the end of this session.

## Where the project stands

**Phases 0 and 1 done. First Phase 2 milestone (next-step valid-edge rate) also done.**

Concretely: the data pipeline, model, training loop, and edge-validity evaluator
are all written, committed, and demonstrated end-to-end on the City of London
corpus. The trained model has learned London's street adjacency from nothing but
sequences of integer intersection IDs, validated against the ground-truth graph.

5 (causal intervention), 6 (visualization & write-up) — is still ahead.

### Model + eval scaffolding (commit `032a866`)

- `model/model.py` — nanoGPT-style decoder-only transformer with weight-tied
  output head, PAD-masked cross-entropy, dtype assertions enforcing THE ONE
  RULE on every batch. Heavy comments + ASCII diagram of the forward pass.
- `model/configs/small.py` — ~11M-param smoke config
  (`block_size=256, n_layer=6, n_head=6, n_embd=384`, `max_iters=2000`).
- `model/configs/medium.py` — ~25–30M-param full-scale config
  (`block_size=512, n_layer=8, n_head=8, n_embd=512, max_iters=30000`).
- `model/train.py` — training loop with AdamW + cosine LR + grad clip,
  optional AMP on CUDA, per-batch in-bounds check on token IDs, val/gen
  perplexity logging every `eval_interval`, best-val checkpoint that stores
  enough state (config dict + vocab_size) for eval scripts to rebuild the
  model from the file alone.
- `eval/valid_edge.py` — the "did the model learn the city graph?" evaluator.
  Mode A: next-step valid-edge rate (Othello-GPT legal-move-rate analogue).
  Mode B: full-route generation validity. Neither touches `coords.csv` —
  only graph adjacency.

### First end-to-end training run — City of London

- Hardware: M1 Pro, 32 GB RAM, PyTorch 2.8 with MPS backend.
- Calibration: 566 ms/iter with `small.py` settings on London.
- Run: `python -u model/train.py --config model/configs/small.py
  --data_dir data/london_city --seed 0`
- Wall time: ~22 minutes.

Training trajectory (cross-entropy loss / perplexity):

| Iter | train CE | train ppl | **val CE** | **val ppl** | gen CE | gen ppl |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
|    0 |    6.57 |  712.0 |     —   |     —   |    —   |    —   |
|  100 |    1.85 |    6.4 |     —   |     —   |    —   |    —   |
|  500 |    0.69 |    1.99 |   0.535 |   1.71  |   0.657 |   1.93 |
| 1000 |    0.65 |    1.92 |   0.509 |   1.66  |   0.679 |   1.97 |
| 1500 |    0.64 |    1.89 | **0.502** | **1.65** |   0.715 |   2.04 |
| final |   0.62 |    1.86 |   0.502 |   1.65  |   0.758 |   2.13 |

- Best val perplexity **1.65** (saved checkpoint at iter 1500, 42 MB).
- Train ppl > val ppl throughout — expected because `train.bin` mixes random
  walks (high entropy, no destination signal) with shortest paths, while
  `val.bin` is pure shortest paths.
- Gen perplexity drifted up (1.93 → 2.13) while val stayed flat — a mild
  generalization gap to held-out destinations. Small in absolute terms but
  worth tracking on larger cities.

### Edge-validation results — City of London

`eval/valid_edge.py` against the saved checkpoint:

| Metric | Val split | Gen split (held-out destinations) | Full-route |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Valid-edge rate (any real neighbor)   | **99.69%** | **99.30%** |  **99.76%** |
| Top-1 accuracy (exact label match)    | 91.69%     | 90.74%     |       —     |
| Fully-valid 80-step routes (200 sampled) |   —     |    —      |  **92.50%** |

Reference baseline (untrained model, random init): valid-edge rate ≈
`avg_out_degree / vocab_size` ≈ 2 / 666 ≈ **0.3%**.

The 99.30% on the gen split — routes whose destinations were never the
endpoint of any training route — is the more interesting number: it shows the
model learned graph structure rather than memorizing endpoints.

## What is not yet shown

This session's work establishes that the model has learned London's
**adjacency**. It does not yet show:

- That a **metric map** of London is recoverable from the model's
  activations (Phase 4, linear probe).
- That the map is encoded **linearly** rather than nonlinearly (Phase 4,
  linear-vs-MLP probe comparison).
- That the recovered geometry came from **training** rather than from probe
  capacity (Phase 4, untrained / raw-embedding / destroyed-structure
  controls).
- That the internal location representation **causally drives** the model's
  routing (Phase 5, activation patching).

These are the substantive open questions the project exists to answer. The
work this session was the necessary scaffolding to ask them.
